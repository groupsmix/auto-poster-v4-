Category: Courses & E-Learning
Specific rules:
- Buyers invest in outcomes, not content volume -- lead with "After this course, you will be able to..."
- Curriculum structure signals quality: clear modules, progressive difficulty, estimated completion time
- Instructor credibility is essential: mention relevant experience, results achieved, students taught
- Price range: $19-$49 for mini-courses, $97-$297 for comprehensive programs, $497+ for premium with coaching
- Video is the expected format -- mention total video hours, downloadable resources, and lifetime access
- Completion certificates increase perceived value, especially for professional development topics
- Keywords: "[skill] course", "learn [topic] online", "[topic] masterclass", "[skill] certification"
- Student testimonials with specific outcomes ("landed my first client in 2 weeks") convert powerfully
- Drip content vs. full access: mention which model is used -- full access is preferred for digital products
- Platform considerations: Udemy (high traffic, low price), Teachable/Gumroad (higher price, own audience)

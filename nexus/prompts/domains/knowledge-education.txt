Domain context: Knowledge & Education
Key facts:
- Buyers invest in transformation and outcomes -- sell the skill they will gain, not the hours of content
- Credibility signals are critical: instructor expertise, student results, testimonials, certifications
- Course structure matters: clear learning path, progressive difficulty, measurable milestones
- Completion rates are low industry-wide -- design for engagement with bite-sized modules and quick wins
- Pricing tiers work well: free lead magnet -> low-cost intro course -> premium comprehensive program
- Udemy, Skillshare, Teachable, and Gumroad are primary distribution channels with different audience expectations
- Video courses outperform text-only by 3-5x in perceived value, but well-designed workbooks add significant value
- Community access and live Q&A sessions dramatically increase perceived value and reduce refund rates
- Evergreen content with periodic updates outperforms time-sensitive material for long-term passive income
- Keywords should target "how to [skill]", "learn [skill]", "[skill] course", "[skill] certification"

Domain context: Specialized Technology (AI, Cybersecurity, PropTech, DeepTech)
Key facts:
- Buyers are technically sophisticated -- generic marketing language destroys credibility instantly
- Domain expertise must be demonstrated through specific technical details, not buzzwords
- AI/ML products: emphasize practical applications, integration ease, and measurable ROI over raw model performance
- Cybersecurity products: compliance frameworks (SOC2, GDPR, HIPAA), threat models, and audit readiness sell
- PropTech products: focus on time savings, cost reduction, regulatory compliance, and portfolio scalability
- Technical documentation quality directly correlates with product perceived value and adoption
- Open-source alternatives exist for most tools -- differentiation must come from UX, support, or specialization
- B2B pricing models dominate: per-seat, usage-based, or tiered SaaS pricing with enterprise tiers
- Case studies with quantifiable results (ROI, time saved, incidents prevented) outperform feature lists
- Early adopters in tech niches are vocal advocates -- optimize for word-of-mouth and community building
